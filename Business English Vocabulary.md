

Career prospects
?
карьерные перспективы; шансы на успех и развитие в профессии в будущем.
- Definition (EN): your chances to develop and be successful in your career in the future.
- Example: I took this job because the career prospects are much better.
- Word family: career, career path, career development.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/career-prospects

Job satisfaction
?
удовлетворённость работой; ощущение, что твоя работа важна и приносит результат.
- Definition (EN): the pleasure and sense of achievement you feel when your job is worth doing.
- Example: High job satisfaction often leads to better performance.
- Word family: satisfied, satisfying, dissatisfaction.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/job-satisfaction

Occupation
?
занятие, профессия; то, чем человек регулярно занимается.
- Definition (EN): your regular job or profession; also an activity you spend time doing.
- Example: Teaching is a very demanding occupation.
- Word family: occupational, occupy, occupation (military control of a place).
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/occupation

Promotion
?
повышение; перевод на более высокую должность.
- Definition (EN): a move to a more important or better-paid job in the same company.
- Example: She got a promotion to sales manager last year.
- Word family: promote, promotional, promotion prospects.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/promotion

Qualification
?
квалификация; образование, навыки или сертификаты, которые позволяют выполнять работу.
- Definition (EN): skills, knowledge or exams that show you are able to do a job.
- Example: A teaching qualification is required for this position.
- Word family: qualify, qualified, disqualified.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/qualification

Job security
?
гарантия занятости; уверенность, что работу не потеряешь.
- Definition (EN): how likely it is that you can keep your job in the future.
- Example: Many people prefer job security to a higher salary.
- Word family: secure, security, job insecurity.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/job-security

Rent-free accommodation
?
жильё без аренды; проживание без оплаты за квартиру/дом.
- Definition (EN): a place to live where you do not have to pay rent.
- Example: The company provides rent-free accommodation for junior staff.
- Word family: rent, rental, tenant, landlord.
- Cambridge: rent-free — https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/rent-free ; accommodation — https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/accommodation

Paid time off (work)
?
оплачиваемый отпуск / оплачиваемое свободное время от работы.
- Definition (EN): time when you are not working but still receive your normal pay.
- Example: Employees get 25 days of paid time off per year.
- Word family: time off, day off, unpaid leave.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/time-off

Working conditions
?
условия труда; всё, что связано со средой на работе (зарплата, часы, безопасность и т.п.).
- Definition (EN): all the things that influence your work environment, such as pay, hours and safety.
- Example: Good working conditions are a key factor in job satisfaction.
- Word family: work, workplace, work environment.
- Cambridge: working — https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/working ; conditions — https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/conditions

Receive / receives
?
receive / receives — получать, принимать (что-то, что тебе дают или отправляют).
- Grammar: **receives** = he/she/it receives (3rd person singular, Present Simple).
- Definition (EN): to get or be given something.
- Example: She receives a bonus at the end of each year.
- Word family: receive, received, receiving, receiver, receipt.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/receive

pleasure — удовольствие, наслаждение, чувство удовлетворения.

- Word family: pleasant, pleasurable, displeasure.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/pleasure

Put them in the order

put (them) in order — расположить в правильной последовательности / привести в порядок.

flock (to) — стекаться, массово приходить/приезжать куда-то.

stand out — выделяться, бросаться в глаза на фоне других.

Privately
?
privately — лично, наедине; не публично; иногда: за свой счёт / в частном порядке.
- Definition (EN): in secret or with only one or two other people; not in public.
- Example: Can we talk privately after the meeting?
- Word family: private, privacy, privately owned.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/privately

Business executive
?
business executive — руководитель компании; человек на высокой управленческой позиции.
- Definition (EN): someone in a senior position in business who makes important decisions and manages others.
- Example: Many business executives work long hours and travel a lot.
- Word family: executive, senior executive, C-level executive.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/executive

Redundant
?
redundant — (BrE, HR) сокращённый; потерявший работу, потому что компания больше не нуждается в этой должности.
- Definition (EN): having lost your job because your employer no longer needs that job to be done.
- Example: He was made redundant when the factory closed.
- Extra meaning: also “unnecessary, more than is needed” (e.g. redundant information).
- Word family: redundancy, redundancies.
- Cambridge: https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/redundant
